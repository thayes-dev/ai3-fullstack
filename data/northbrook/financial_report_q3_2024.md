# Northbrook Partners — Quarterly Financial Report

**Period:** Q3 2024 (July 1 - September 30, 2024)
**Prepared By:** James Wright, Chief Financial Officer
**Date:** October 10, 2024
**Department:** Finance
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential

---

## Executive Summary

Northbrook Partners delivered strong financial performance in Q3 2024, posting revenue of $12.4 million and net income of $2.6 million. Revenue grew 8% year-over-year, driven by expanded client engagements and the successful onboarding of three new enterprise accounts. The company remains on track to meet or exceed the full-year revenue target of $47 million.

---

## Revenue

| Metric | Q3 2024 | Q3 2023 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Total Revenue | $12.4M | $11.5M | +8.0% |
| Recurring Revenue | $7.8M | $7.1M | +9.9% |
| Project-Based Revenue | $4.6M | $4.4M | +4.5% |

Revenue growth was broad-based across the client portfolio. The financial services sector continues to be the largest revenue contributor at 34% of total revenue, followed by healthcare (22%), technology (19%), manufacturing (14%), and other sectors (11%).

Three new enterprise clients were onboarded during the quarter: a regional healthcare network, a mid-market financial services firm, and a technology startup scaling its operations. Combined initial contract values total $1.1 million, with potential for expansion as engagements deepen.

Year-to-date revenue through Q3 stands at **$34.6 million**, representing 74% of the annual target.

---

## Expenses

| Category | Q3 2024 | % of Revenue |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Payroll & Benefits | $6.08M | 49.0% |
| Contractor & Consulting | $1.57M | 12.7% |
| Technology & Infrastructure | $0.78M | 6.3% |
| Facilities & Operations | $0.65M | 5.2% |
| Sales & Marketing | $0.42M | 3.4% |
| General & Administrative | $0.30M | 2.4% |
| **Total Operating Expenses** | **$9.80M** | **79.0%** |

Total operating expenses for Q3 were **$9.8 million**, an increase of 11% over Q3 2023 ($8.8M). The primary driver of the increase was payroll, which grew due to the engineering hiring ramp approved in early 2024. Payroll as a percentage of total operating expenses was **62%**, slightly above the target of 60%. This ratio is expected to normalize as newly hired engineers become fully billable in Q4.

Technology and infrastructure costs increased 18% year-over-year, reflecting ongoing investment in the cloud migration project and new development tooling. This spending is expected to decrease once the migration is complete in mid-2025.

## Profitability

| Metric | Q3 2024 | Q3 2023 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Gross Margin | 79.0% | 80.2% |
| Net Income | $2.6M | $2.7M |
| Net Margin | 21.0% | 23.5% |

Net income for Q3 was **$2.6 million**, representing a net margin of 21.0%. While the net margin declined slightly compared to Q3 2023 (23.5%), this is attributable to the planned investment in headcount and infrastructure. Management views this as a strategic trade-off that positions the company for accelerated growth in 2025.

## Outlook

Based on current pipeline activity, Q4 revenue is projected to reach $13.5-$14.5 million. Several large project deliverables are scheduled for completion in Q4, which will drive both revenue recognition and client satisfaction. The full-year revenue target of $47 million is within reach, with potential upside to $49 million if pipeline conversions close as expected.

Key risks for Q4 include delayed project starts due to client budget cycles and the holiday season impact on billable hours in December.
