# Northbrook Partners — Internal Memo

**To:** All Employees
**From:** Rachel Kim, VP of Human Resources
**Date:** February 5, 2025
**Subject:** Correction: Updated PTO Carryover Cap — Effective Date Clarification

---

Team,

I am writing to clarify an important detail regarding the new Paid Time Off policy (HR-2025-004) that was announced in January 2025. Since the rollout, several employees and managers have raised questions about when the updated vacation carryover cap takes effect, and I want to make sure everyone has the correct information.

## Carryover Cap: What Is Actually in Effect Right Now

There has been confusion about whether the new **10-day carryover cap** applies to vacation days carried into 2025. To be clear:

**The new 10-day carryover cap does NOT take effect until January 1, 2026.** For the 2025 calendar year, the previous **4-day carryover limit** from HR-2023-004 remains in effect. This means that any vacation days you carried from 2024 into 2025 were subject to the old 4-day maximum, and any days you carry from 2025 into 2026 will be the first year governed by the new 10-day cap.

This transition period was designed to prevent employees who planned their 2024 time off based on the old policy from losing accrued days unexpectedly. We recognized that changing the carryover rules mid-cycle would be unfair to anyone who had already made decisions about when to use or save their vacation time.

## Affected Employees

Our records show that **14 employees** currently have carryover balances exceeding the old 4-day limit. These balances were honored under the transition provisions outlined in the new policy. HR will reach out individually to each of these employees to discuss options before the 2026 cap takes effect. If you believe you may be affected and have not heard from us by February 21, please contact HR directly.

## Recharge Day Preference Deadline Extended

I also want to flag a second correction. The originally announced deadline for submitting 2025 Recharge Day preferences was **January 31**. This has been extended to **February 28** to allow time for the updated policy FAQ to be published. If you have already submitted your preferences, no action is needed. If you have not, you now have until the end of February.

## Updated FAQ

The updated PTO FAQ is available on the HR portal under **Policies > Time Off > FAQ** (last updated February 3, 2025). The FAQ addresses the most common questions we have received, including carryover timing, Recharge Day scheduling, and how the unlimited sick day provision works in practice.

I apologize for any confusion the initial rollout may have caused. Our goal is to make sure every employee understands their benefits and can plan accordingly. If you have questions that are not covered in the FAQ, please do not hesitate to reach out.

Rachel
