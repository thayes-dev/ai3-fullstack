# Northbrook Partners — Internal Memo

**To:** All Employees
**From:** Maria Rodriguez, CTO
**Date:** January 22, 2025
**Subject:** Updated Security Protocols — Action Required by February 28, 2025

---

Team,

As part of our ongoing commitment to protecting Northbrook Partners' systems, client data, and intellectual property, we are implementing several updated security protocols effective immediately. Some of these changes require action on your part, so please read this memo carefully and complete the required steps by **February 28, 2025**.

## Why Now

In Q4 2024, our security team completed an external audit of our IT infrastructure. The audit identified several areas where we can strengthen our defenses, particularly around authentication, network access, and credential management. None of the findings indicated an active breach or compromise, but the recommendations align with industry best practices and the requirements of several client contracts that mandate specific security standards.

Additionally, as we prepare for the office relocation to 250 Innovation Drive in March, we are taking the opportunity to modernize our security infrastructure to match the new environment.

## What Is Changing

### 1. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) — Required by February 15, 2025

**All employees must enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on their Northbrook Partners account by February 15, 2025.** MFA adds a second layer of verification beyond your password, typically a code generated by an authenticator app on your phone.

We support the following MFA methods:
- **Recommended:** Authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Authy)
- **Acceptable:** Hardware security key (YubiKey or similar FIDO2 device)
- **Not supported:** SMS-based codes (due to known vulnerabilities)

Step-by-step setup instructions are available on the IT support page of the intranet. If you need assistance, IT will be holding drop-in clinics on February 4, 6, and 11 from 12:00 PM to 2:00 PM in Conference Room C.

**After February 15, accounts without MFA enabled will be restricted to in-office network access only until MFA is configured.**

### 2. New VPN Provider

We are transitioning from our current VPN provider (SecureLink) to **Cloudflare Zero Trust** as our new remote access solution. The new VPN offers faster connection speeds, better reliability, and integration with our MFA system.

The transition timeline:
- **January 27:** New VPN client available for download via the IT self-service portal.
- **February 3-14:** Dual-operation period — both old and new VPN will work.
- **February 17:** Old VPN (SecureLink) will be decommissioned. Only the new VPN will function after this date.

IT will push the new VPN client to company-issued laptops automatically during the week of January 27. Employees using personal devices for remote work must install the client manually using the instructions on the IT support page.

### 3. Password Rotation Policy

Effective immediately, all employees must update their Northbrook Partners password **every 90 days**. The system will prompt you automatically when your password is due for rotation. Passwords must meet the following requirements:

- Minimum 14 characters (increased from 12)
- At least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character
- Cannot reuse any of your last 10 passwords
- Cannot contain your name, email address, or common dictionary words

We strongly recommend using a password manager. Northbrook Partners provides enterprise licenses for **1Password** — if you do not already have access, contact IT to be added.

### 4. Device Compliance

Starting March 1, 2025, all devices connecting to company systems (including personal devices used for remote work) must meet updated compliance requirements:

- Operating system must be within 2 versions of current (e.g., macOS 14+ or Windows 11 22H2+)
- Automatic security updates must be enabled
- Disk encryption must be active (FileVault on Mac, BitLocker on Windows)
- Company-approved antivirus must be installed and current
- Screen lock must activate after 5 minutes of inactivity

Devices that do not meet these requirements will be blocked from accessing company systems after March 1. IT can help you verify compliance — use the self-check tool on the IT support page or visit during drop-in hours.
