# Northbrook Partners — Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Policy Number:** FIN-2024-011
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024
**Department:** Finance
**Approved By:** James Wright, CFO

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the reimbursement of business-related expenses incurred by Northbrook Partners employees. All employees are expected to exercise sound judgment and fiscal responsibility when incurring expenses on behalf of the company.

## General Guidelines

Expenses must be directly related to Northbrook Partners business and must be reasonable and necessary. All reimbursement requests require itemized receipts. Credit card statements alone are not sufficient documentation. Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred; submissions beyond 60 days may be denied at the discretion of the Finance department.

Reimbursements are processed on a bi-weekly cycle aligned with payroll. Approved expenses submitted by the 1st and 15th of each month will be included in the next payroll run.

## Meal and Entertainment Expenses

Employees traveling on business or hosting clients may expense meals up to **$65 per day**. This daily limit applies to the total of breakfast, lunch, and dinner combined. Alcohol is limited to $25 per person per event when entertaining clients and must be noted separately on the expense report.

For team meals and celebrations, a per-person cap of **$50** applies. Manager pre-approval is required for team events exceeding 8 attendees.

**First Friday Team Lunch:** Each team receives a **$45 per person** budget on the first Friday of each month for a team meal. No pre-approval is required. The manager submits a single receipt through the Finance portal.

Tips should be reasonable and customary (15-20%) and are included in the daily meal cap.

## Travel Expenses

### Air Travel

All air travel must be booked in economy class. Employees may book premium economy for flights exceeding 6 hours with manager approval. Business class requires VP-level approval and is generally reserved for international travel exceeding 8 hours.

**Pre-approval is required for any flight costing more than $500.** Pre-approval requests should be submitted through the Finance portal at least 10 business days before travel. Employees are expected to book flights at least 14 days in advance to secure reasonable fares.

### Ground Transportation

Rental cars should be mid-size or smaller unless business needs require a larger vehicle. Employees must decline the rental company's insurance, as Northbrook Partners maintains a corporate policy covering rental vehicles.

Rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) are reimbursable for business-related transportation. Rideshare surge pricing above 1.8x is not reimbursable. Employees should wait for surge to subside, use public transit, or take a standard taxi.

Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at a tiered rate: **$0.58 per mile for the first 200 miles per month** and **$0.45 per mile thereafter**. Mileage logs must include date, origin, destination, business purpose, and total miles.

### Lodging

Hotel stays should be booked at mid-range properties. The nightly rate guideline is $200 for most domestic markets and **$275 per night for New York City, San Francisco, Washington DC, and Chicago**. Rates above these guidelines require manager pre-approval.

## Equipment and Supplies

Office supplies under $50 do not require pre-approval. Technology purchases (software licenses, peripherals, etc.) exceeding $100 require manager approval. Purchases over $500 require Finance department approval.

## Client Appreciation Gifts

Employees may expense client appreciation gifts up to **$125 per client per quarter**. All gifts must be logged in the CRM with a written business justification. Gift cards and cash equivalents are prohibited.

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are not eligible for reimbursement: personal entertainment, gym memberships, clothing (unless required safety equipment), fines or traffic violations, airline upgrades purchased without approval, and expenses for companions or family members traveling with the employee.

## Submitting Expense Reports

All expense reports must be submitted through the Finance portal with the following information: date of expense, vendor name, business purpose, itemized receipt, and project or cost center code. Managers must review and approve direct reports' expense submissions within 5 business days.

**The "Sunset Rule":** Expense reports not submitted within the 30-day window are automatically declined. Exceptions require VP-level approval and a written explanation submitted to finance@northbrookpartners.com.

## Policy Violations

Fraudulent expense claims or repeated policy violations will result in disciplinary action, including potential termination and repayment of improperly claimed amounts.
