# Northbrook Partners — Quarterly Financial Report

**Period:** Q4 2024 (October 1 - December 31, 2024)
**Prepared By:** James Wright, Chief Financial Officer
**Date:** January 15, 2025
**Department:** Finance
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential

---

## Executive Summary

Q4 2024 was the strongest quarter in Northbrook Partners' history. Revenue reached $14.1 million, a 14% increase over Q4 2023, driven by project completions, expanded enterprise engagements, and continued momentum in the healthcare and technology sectors. Net income for the quarter was $2.9 million. For the full year, total revenue was $48.7 million, exceeding the annual target of $47 million by 3.6%.

---

## Revenue

| Metric | Q4 2024 | Q4 2023 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Total Revenue | $14.1M | $12.4M | +14.0% |
| Recurring Revenue | $8.9M | $7.6M | +17.1% |
| Project-Based Revenue | $5.2M | $4.8M | +8.3% |

The strong quarter was driven by the completion of several large client engagements that had been in progress since Q2, as well as contract renewals with expanded scope from 4 existing enterprise clients. Recurring revenue growth of 17.1% reflects the company's ongoing shift toward longer-term engagement models.

### Full-Year Revenue Summary

| Quarter | Revenue |
|---------|---------|
| Q1 2024 | $10.8M |
| Q2 2024 | $11.4M |
| Q3 2024 | $12.4M |
| Q4 2024 | $14.1M |
| **Full Year 2024** | **$48.7M** |

Full-year revenue of **$48.7 million** represents 18% growth over 2023 ($41.3M) and exceeds the board-approved target of $47 million. This performance positions the company well for the 2025 revenue target of $60 million.

---

## Expenses

| Category | Q4 2024 | % of Revenue |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Payroll & Benefits | $7.06M | 50.1% |
| Contractor & Consulting | $1.41M | 10.0% |
| Technology & Infrastructure | $0.92M | 6.5% |
| Facilities & Operations | $0.85M | 6.0% |
| Sales & Marketing | $0.56M | 4.0% |
| General & Administrative | $0.40M | 2.8% |
| **Total Operating Expenses** | **$11.20M** | **79.4%** |

Total operating expenses for Q4 were **$11.2 million**. The increase over Q3 ($9.8M) is primarily due to year-end bonuses ($1.1M accrual), continued hiring costs, and initial expenses related to the office relocation planning. Payroll remains the dominant cost category at 63% of total operating expenses, reflecting the people-intensive nature of the business.

Facilities costs increased in Q4 due to the deposit and early planning costs for the new office at 250 Innovation Drive. These are one-time costs and will normalize once the move is complete.

## Profitability

| Metric | Q4 2024 | Q4 2023 |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Gross Margin | 79.4% | 80.0% |
| Net Income | $2.9M | $2.5M |
| Net Margin | 20.6% | 20.2% |

Net income for Q4 was **$2.9 million**, up 16% from Q4 2023. Despite higher expenses, the strong revenue performance maintained a healthy net margin of 20.6%.

### Full-Year Profitability

| Metric | 2024 | 2023 |
|--------|------|------|
| Total Revenue | $48.7M | $41.3M |
| Total Expenses | $39.0M | $33.1M |
| Net Income | $9.8M | $8.2M |

Full-year net income was **$9.8 million**, a 19.5% increase over 2023. The net margin of 20.1% is the highest in company history and reflects the operating leverage achieved through revenue growth outpacing expense growth.

Client retention improved to 94%, with only 3 clients lost during the year (2 due to acquisition, 1 due to budget constraints). The increase in average revenue per client reflects deeper engagement models and successful upsell efforts.

---

## 2025 Outlook

Management is targeting **$60 million in revenue for 2025**, representing approximately 23% growth over 2024. This target is supported by a strong existing client base (94% retention), a robust pipeline of new opportunities, the planned market expansion into 2-3 new geographies, and the launch of AI-augmented service offerings.

Key investments for 2025 include:
- AI initiative: $2.1M (approved by board, January 2025)
- Office relocation to 250 Innovation Drive: estimated $800K in one-time costs
- Continued engineering hiring: 6 remaining positions plus 3 new AI roles

Key risks include economic uncertainty, the execution risk associated with the AI initiative, and the operational disruption of the office move.
