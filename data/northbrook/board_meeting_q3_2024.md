## 1. Q3 2024 Financial Summary

James Wright presented the Q3 2024 financial results. Total revenue for the quarter was **$12.4 million**, representing an **8% increase** over Q3 2023 ($11.5M). The growth was driven primarily by expanded engagements with existing clients in the financial services and healthcare sectors. Three new enterprise clients were onboarded during the quarter, contributing approximately $1.1M in combined revenue.

Operating expenses for Q3 totaled **$9.8 million**, resulting in a net income of **$2.6 million**. Payroll continues to be the largest expense category at 62% of total operating costs. James noted that the payroll ratio is slightly above the target of 60% due to the hiring ramp in Engineering but is expected to normalize as new hires become fully billable in Q4.

Year-to-date revenue through Q3 stands at **$34.6 million**, and the company remains on track to meet the annual target of $47 million. Cash reserves are healthy at $8.2 million, and the company has no outstanding debt.

The board reviewed and accepted the financial report.

## 2. Operational Update

David Park provided an overview of Q3 operations. Client satisfaction scores averaged 4.3 out of 5 across all active engagements, consistent with the prior quarter. The project delivery on-time rate improved to 91% from 87% in Q2, attributed to the new project management tooling rolled out in August.

Employee headcount at the end of Q3 was 308, up from 294 at the start of the quarter. Voluntary turnover for the quarter was 3.2%, below the industry average of 4.5%. David highlighted that the new onboarding program, launched in July, has received positive feedback from recent hires.

## 3. New Office Lease Proposal

David Park presented a proposal to relocate the Chicago headquarters from the current location at 100 Main Street to a larger space at 250 Innovation Drive. The proposed space offers 45,000 square feet (compared to the current 32,000) and is located in a newer building with modern amenities, including a dedicated event space and improved parking.

The proposed lease terms are 7 years at $38 per square foot, with a 6-month build-out period funded by the landlord up to $500,000 in tenant improvements. The estimated move date is March 2025. David noted that the current lease at 100 Main Street expires in June 2025, providing adequate overlap.

After discussion regarding the financial impact and growth projections, the board **approved the new office lease** with a vote of 5-0. Sarah Chen noted that the larger space positions the company for headcount growth through 2027.

## 4. Engineering Headcount

Maria Rodriguez presented a request to **hire 15 additional engineers** over the next two quarters to support the growing demand for technology-enabled client solutions, the ongoing Project Atlas modernization effort, and anticipated engineering needs for the AI initiative proposed for 2025. The roles include 8 senior engineers, 4 mid-level engineers, and 3 junior engineers.

The estimated annual cost impact is $2.8 million in total compensation. Maria emphasized that current engineering capacity is at 94% utilization, which creates risk for delivery timelines and limits the team's ability to take on new projects. James Wright confirmed that the budget can absorb the additional headcount given current revenue trends.

The board **approved the engineering hiring plan** with a vote of 5-0.

## 5. Strategic Initiatives Update

Sarah Chen provided an update on the company's three strategic initiatives for 2024:

- **Client Diversification:** On track. Revenue concentration from the top 5 clients decreased from 48% to 41% year-over-year.
- **Operational Excellence:** On track. New project management and time-tracking tools deployed; employee satisfaction survey scores improved 6 points.
- **Technology Investment:** In progress. Project Atlas — our platform modernization initiative — is 40% complete, with expected completion in Q2 2025.

## 6. Open Discussion

Board member Robert Tanaka asked about the company's plans for AI adoption. Sarah Chen indicated that a formal AI strategy would be presented at the Q4 board meeting, including a proposed budget for an AI initiative in 2025.

No further business was raised.
