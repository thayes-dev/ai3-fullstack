# Account Security — Passwords, MFA, and Suspicious Activity

## Password Requirements
- Minimum 12 characters
- Must include uppercase, lowercase, number, and special character
- Cannot reuse your last 10 passwords
- Passwords expire every 90 days

## How to Change Your Password
1. Go to https://sso.northbrookpartners.com/account
2. Click "Change Password"
3. Enter your current password, then your new password twice
4. Click "Update" — changes take effect immediately across all Northbrook systems

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
All employees must have MFA enabled. Northbrook uses Microsoft Authenticator.
- If you get an MFA push you didn't initiate: **DENY it immediately** and change your password
- If you're locked out of MFA: contact the Security team with a photo ID for identity verification

## Responding to Suspicious Activity

### If You Receive a Suspicious Email
1. Do NOT click any links or download attachments
2. Forward the email to phishing@northbrookpartners.com
3. Delete the email from your inbox
4. If you DID click a link or enter credentials: immediately change your password and contact Security

### If You See an Unauthorized Login Alert
1. Change your password immediately at sso.northbrookpartners.com
2. Review your recent login activity (SSO portal → "Activity Log")
3. Report the incident to security@northbrookpartners.com with:
   - The alert details (time, IP address, location)
   - Whether you recognize the login attempt
   - Any sensitive data your account can access
4. Security will investigate and may temporarily restrict your account as a precaution

### Signs Your Account May Be Compromised
- MFA push notifications you didn't request
- Emails in your "Sent" folder you didn't write
- Colleagues receiving messages from you that you didn't send
- Unexpected password change notifications

**Response SLA:** Security incidents are treated as critical. Target response: 30 minutes during business hours, 2 hours after hours.
