## 1. Q4 2024 Financial Summary and Year-End Review

James Wright presented the Q4 2024 financials and year-end summary. Q4 revenue was **$14.1 million**, representing a **14% increase** over Q4 2023 ($12.4M). This marked the strongest quarter in company history and was driven by several large project completions and the successful ramp of newly onboarded enterprise clients.

Q4 operating expenses were **$11.2 million**, yielding a net income of **$2.9 million**. The increase in expenses was attributed to the engineering hiring ramp approved in Q3, year-end bonuses, and costs associated with the upcoming office relocation.

For the full year 2024, Northbrook Partners reported **total revenue of $48.7 million**, exceeding the annual target of $47 million by 3.6%. Full-year net income was **$9.8 million**, representing a 20.1% net margin. James noted that the company's financial position is the strongest it has been since founding, with cash reserves of $9.4 million and zero debt.

The board reviewed and accepted the year-end financial report.

## 2. Client Retention and Growth Metrics

David Park presented client metrics for 2024. The annual client retention rate was **94%**, up from 91% in 2023. Of the clients lost, two were due to acquisition by larger firms and one was due to budget constraints unrelated to Northbrook's services.

The net promoter score (NPS) across the client portfolio was 62, which David noted is well above the professional services industry average of 40. Twelve new enterprise clients were added during the year, contributing $8.3 million in combined revenue.

David highlighted that the client diversification initiative is ahead of schedule. Revenue concentration from the top 5 clients is now 38%, down from 48% at the start of 2024.

## 3. AI Initiative Proposal

Sarah Chen and Maria Rodriguez jointly presented Project Meridian — the company's proposed AI initiative for 2025. The proposal has three pillars:

**Pillar 1 — AI-Augmented Client Services:** Develop AI-powered tools to accelerate research, analysis, and reporting workflows that Northbrook delivers to clients. Target: reduce delivery time for standard analysis engagements by 30%.

**Pillar 2 — Internal Productivity:** Deploy AI tools for internal use, including automated document drafting, meeting summarization, and code review assistance for the Engineering team. Target: save 4,000 employee hours per year.

**Pillar 3 — AI Practice Development:** Begin building a dedicated AI consulting practice to serve clients who need guidance on AI strategy, implementation, and governance. Target: generate $2M in AI-related revenue by end of 2026.

The proposed **Project Meridian budget is $2.1 million** for 2025, broken down as:
- Personnel (2 senior AI engineers, 1 AI product manager): $850K
- Infrastructure and tooling (API costs, compute, platforms): $600K
- Training and upskilling (existing staff): $350K
- External consulting and partnerships: $300K

Maria Rodriguez will lead the initiative with Kevin Okafor overseeing the engineering execution. A quarterly review cadence was proposed to track ROI and adjust investment as needed.

After extensive discussion about market timing, competitive positioning, and risk, the board **approved the Project Meridian budget of $2.1 million** with a vote of 5-0. Board member Angela Whitfield emphasized the importance of establishing clear metrics for each pillar within the first 90 days. Following her recommendation, the board established a "Leverage Ratio" target of 1.8 — hours saved per dollar invested — as Project Meridian's primary success metric.

## 4. 2025 Strategic Priorities

Sarah Chen outlined the company's three strategic priorities for 2025:

1. **AI-First Services:** Execute the AI initiative and begin generating measurable client value.
2. **Market Expansion:** Open a presence in two to three new markets (Seattle, Atlanta, and Boston are under evaluation).
3. **Revenue Target:** Achieve **$60 million in annual revenue**, representing 23% growth over 2024.

Sarah noted that these priorities are ambitious but achievable given the company's current momentum and financial position. Detailed plans for each priority will be shared with the board at the next quarterly meeting.

## 5. Open Discussion

Kevin Okafor provided a brief update on the engineering team, noting that 9 of the 15 approved hires from Q3 have been filled. The remaining 6 positions are expected to close by end of Q1 2025. Project Atlas — the cloud infrastructure migration — is 55% complete.

Board member Linda Foster asked about the office relocation timeline. David Park confirmed the move to 250 Innovation Drive is on schedule for March 2025 and that employee communications will begin in February.

No further business was raised.
