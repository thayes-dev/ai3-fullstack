# Northbrook Partners — Paid Time Off Policy

**Policy Number:** HR-2025-004
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Department:** Human Resources
**Approved By:** Sarah Chen, CEO
**Supersedes:** HR-2023-004

---

## Purpose

Northbrook Partners is committed to supporting employee well-being through generous and flexible time-off benefits. Following an extensive review by the HR Committee in Q4 2024, this updated policy reflects our investment in employee health, retention, and work-life balance. This policy replaces the previous PTO policy (HR-2023-004) effective January 1, 2025.

## Eligibility

All full-time employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period are eligible for PTO benefits under this policy. Part-time employees working 20 or more hours per week receive prorated benefits based on their scheduled hours.

## Paid Time Off Allowances

### Vacation Days

Each eligible full-time employee receives **20 vacation days** per calendar year, an increase from the previous allowance of 17 days. Vacation days accrue on a semi-monthly basis at a rate of approximately 0.833 days per pay period. Unused vacation days may be carried over to the following calendar year, up to a maximum of **10 days** (increased from 4). Any accrued vacation beyond the 10-day carryover limit will be forfeited on January 1 of the new year.

Vacation requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least two weeks in advance. Requests of five or more consecutive days require manager approval and should be submitted at least 30 days in advance.

### Sick Days

Northbrook Partners now offers **unlimited sick days** for all eligible full-time employees. We trust our employees to use sick time responsibly and in good faith. There is no annual cap, and employees are encouraged to prioritize their health without concern for a limited bank of days.

Employees must notify their direct manager by 9:00 AM on any day they intend to use a sick day. For absences of five or more consecutive business days, employees should contact HR to discuss whether short-term disability or FMLA provisions apply.

### Personal Days

Each eligible full-time employee receives **3 personal days** per calendar year, consistent with the previous policy. Personal days are available on January 1 and do not carry over.

### Recharge Days (New for 2025)

Northbrook Partners is introducing **Recharge Days** as a new benefit for 2025. In addition to standard PTO, all employees receive **2 Recharge Days** per calendar year. Recharge Days are company-encouraged mental health days that require no justification or advance notice. Employees simply notify their manager that they are taking a Recharge Day. Recharge Days do not carry over and must be used within the calendar year.

The intent of Recharge Days is to normalize proactive rest. We encourage all employees to take advantage of this benefit.

## Holiday Schedule

Northbrook Partners observes the following paid holidays: New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, the day after Thanksgiving, Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, and a Floating Holiday of the employee's choice. The Floating Holiday must be scheduled at least one week in advance and approved by the employee's manager.

## Bereavement and Jury Duty

Bereavement leave of up to **5 days** is provided for the loss of an immediate family member (spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, sibling, grandparent). This is an increase from 3 days under the previous policy. Jury duty leave is provided as required by law.

## Transition from Previous Policy

Employees who had accrued vacation days under the HR-2023-004 policy will have their balances honored and carried into the new policy framework. The updated carryover maximum of 10 days applies starting January 1, 2026.
