## Welcome to Northbrook Partners

Welcome to Northbrook Partners. Whether you are joining us as a new employee or have been part of the team for years, this handbook serves as your general reference for the values, expectations, and practices that define our workplace. Northbrook Partners was founded on September 14, 2011, with a simple mission: to deliver exceptional professional services through the combination of deep expertise and genuine partnership with our clients. Today, we employ over 320 professionals across offices in Chicago, Denver, and Austin.

Every September 14, we celebrate **Founders Day** — a half-day off (offices close at noon) honoring the company's founding. The day includes the annual Founders Day awards ceremony, where the **Navigator Award** recognizes the employee who best exemplifies cross-team collaboration. Past Navigator Award recipients have their names engraved on the compass plaque displayed in the Chicago lobby.

## Our Values

Northbrook Partners operates according to four core values that guide our decisions, our client relationships, and our interactions with one another:

1. **Integrity First** — We are honest and transparent in all that we do. We own our mistakes and learn from them.
2. **Client Partnership** — We succeed when our clients succeed. We invest in understanding their challenges before proposing solutions.
3. **Continuous Growth** — We believe in lifelong learning. Every employee is encouraged to develop new skills and pursue professional certifications.
4. **Collaborative Excellence** — Great work is a team effort. We share credit generously and support one another across teams and departments.

## Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally and respectfully. Harassment, discrimination, and retaliation of any kind are strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disciplinary action. Northbrook Partners maintains a zero-tolerance policy for workplace violence, threats, and intimidation.

Conflicts of interest must be disclosed to your manager and HR. This includes outside employment, financial interests in client organizations, and personal relationships with direct reports or clients that could impair objectivity.

Confidential information, including client data, financial records, and proprietary processes, must be protected at all times. Employees must not share confidential information outside of authorized business purposes. All employees sign a confidentiality agreement upon hire.

## Onboarding — The Northbrook Navigator Program

Every new hire at Northbrook Partners is enrolled in the **Northbrook Navigator** onboarding program. Upon starting, you will be paired with a peer mentor — called your Navigator — for your first **120 days**. Your Navigator is a colleague outside your direct reporting chain (never your manager) who serves as a go-to resource for questions about culture, tools, processes, and day-to-day life at Northbrook. Navigators receive a **$200 stipend** specifically for team meals with their mentee during the 120-day period. At the end of the program, both the new hire and the Navigator complete a short feedback survey used to improve the program each quarter.

## Dress Code

Northbrook Partners follows a **"Client Mirror"** dress code policy: employees are expected to match the dress standard of whichever client they are serving that week. If a client's office is business professional, dress business professional. If the client environment is casual, dress accordingly. On non-client days, dress is casual — jeans are fine, but no shorts. Clothing with offensive imagery or language is never acceptable.

## Office Hours and Flexibility

Our offices are open from **8:00 AM to 6:00 PM**, Monday through Friday. Employees have flexibility in setting their daily schedule within this window, provided they are available during core hours (10:00 AM to 3:00 PM) and meet their weekly hour commitments. Standard full-time hours are 40 per week.

Northbrook Partners enforces a **No-Meeting Wednesday** policy: no internal meetings may be scheduled on Wednesdays before 2:00 PM. This is enforced via automatic calendar blocks provisioned by IT. Client-facing meetings are exempt from this policy.

Employees working under the hybrid work policy should refer to OPS-2024-007 for specific guidance on remote work days and in-office expectations.

## Performance Reviews

Northbrook Partners conducts **quarterly performance reviews** to ensure ongoing feedback, goal alignment, and professional development. Reviews are conducted by the employee's direct manager using a standardized rubric that covers: quality of work, collaboration, client satisfaction, initiative, and alignment with company values.

Mid-year and year-end reviews are more comprehensive and include self-assessments, peer feedback, and goal-setting for the upcoming period. Annual compensation adjustments are determined following the year-end review cycle in December.

Employees who are underperforming will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) with clear, measurable goals and a 60-day timeline. Support resources, including additional coaching and training, are made available during this period.

## Professional Development

Northbrook Partners invests in employee growth. Each employee has access to an annual professional development budget of **$3,200**, which can be used for conferences, courses, certifications, or books. Employees who present at an external conference or publish a technical article receive an additional **$800** on top of the base budget. Requests should be submitted through the HR portal and approved by the employee's manager.

All employees also receive a **$150 annual "Curiosity Credit"** for books, audiobooks, podcast subscriptions, or museum memberships — no manager approval needed. Submit receipts directly through the Finance portal for reimbursement.

Internal knowledge-sharing sessions, known as **"NB Spark Sessions,"** are held biweekly on Thursdays at noon. Employees are encouraged to both attend and present.

## Questions

For questions about any topic covered in this handbook, please reach out to the HR department at hr@northbrookpartners.com or extension 2100. Your manager is also a valuable first point of contact for day-to-day questions.
