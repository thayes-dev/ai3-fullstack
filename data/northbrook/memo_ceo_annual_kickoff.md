# Northbrook Partners — Internal Memo

**To:** All Employees
**From:** Sarah Chen, CEO
**Date:** January 6, 2025
**Subject:** 2025 Annual Kickoff — Our Year to Lead

---

Team,

Happy New Year. I want to take a moment to reflect on what we accomplished in 2024 and share my vision for where we are headed this year.

## Looking Back: 2024 in Review

Last year was a landmark year for Northbrook Partners. We exceeded our revenue target, hitting **$48.7 million** — an 18% increase over 2023. We welcomed 12 new enterprise clients, maintained a 94% client retention rate, and grew our team to over 320 professionals. These are not just numbers on a slide. They represent the trust our clients place in us and the dedication each of you brings to your work every day.

Some highlights that I am particularly proud of:

- Our client NPS score reached 62, well above the industry average of 40.
- We reduced revenue concentration from our top 5 clients from 48% to 38%, making our business more resilient.
- We launched a new project management platform that improved on-time delivery rates to 91%.
- Our employee engagement score rose to 4.1 out of 5, the highest in company history.

None of this happens without you. Thank you.

## Looking Forward: 2025 Vision

This year, I am asking us to be bold. We have built a strong foundation, and now it is time to lead — not just follow — in how professional services firms leverage technology and talent. Here are the three pillars of our 2025 strategy:

### 1. AI-First Services

Artificial intelligence is not a buzzword for us — it is an operational priority. The board has approved a **$2.1 million investment** in Project Meridian, our AI initiative, which will touch every part of the business. We will build AI-augmented tools to accelerate our client delivery, deploy AI internally to make our teams more productive, and begin developing a dedicated AI consulting practice.

Maria Rodriguez and Kevin Okafor will lead this effort, and you will hear more from them in the coming weeks. The board has set a clear bar for Project Meridian: a Leverage Ratio of 1.8 — meaning for every dollar we invest, we expect to save 1.8 hours of employee effort. I also want to emphasize that this is not about replacing anyone. It is about giving each of you better tools to do your best work. We will be investing heavily in training and upskilling — the 2025 training budget has been increased to **$450,000**, with a significant portion dedicated to AI literacy.

### 2. Market Expansion

We are evaluating expansion into **three new markets**: Seattle, Atlanta, and Boston. These cities have strong client demand, growing talent pools, and strategic alignment with our service areas. Our goal is to establish a presence in at least two of these markets by the end of 2025. David Park is leading the analysis and will share recommendations by the end of Q1.

### 3. Revenue Target: $60 Million

Our 2025 revenue target is **$60 million**. That is 23% growth over 2024, and it is achievable if we execute well across client retention, new client acquisition, and the AI initiative. I am not going to sugarcoat it — this is an ambitious target. But every year for the past four years, this team has met or exceeded its goals. I have no doubt we will do it again.

## What This Means for You

Over the next few weeks, your managers will be sharing team-level goals that align with these company-wide priorities. I encourage you to think about how your work connects to these pillars and to bring ideas to your managers and to me directly. My door — and my inbox — are always open.

On the technology side, Project Atlas — our platform modernization and cloud migration — is over halfway complete and on track for Q2 delivery. This infrastructure work is foundational to everything we are building in 2025, including Project Meridian.

We also have some exciting changes ahead. In March, we will be moving to our new headquarters at **250 Innovation Drive**. The new space is larger, more modern, and better suited to the collaborative, hybrid way we work. David Park will share detailed logistics in February.

I also want to mark your calendars: the first annual **Northbrook Summit** will be held on **April 11** at our new headquarters. The theme is **"Build the Bridge"** — bridging where we are today with where we are going. It will be a full-day event with sessions on our strategic pillars, team showcases, and time to connect across departments. More details to come.

## Closing

I have never been more optimistic about the future of Northbrook Partners. We have the talent, the clients, the financial position, and now the strategic clarity to make 2025 our best year yet.

Let's get after it.

Sarah
