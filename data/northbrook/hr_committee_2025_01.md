## 1. New PTO Policy Rollout

Rachel Kim led the discussion on the rollout of the updated Paid Time Off policy (HR-2025-004), effective January 1, 2025. The new policy increases vacation days from 17 to 20, introduces unlimited sick days, and adds 2 Recharge Days per employee. These changes were approved by the executive team on December 12, 2024, following the committee's recommendation in November.

The rollout plan includes:
- Company-wide email announcement sent on December 20, 2024.
- Updated policy document published on the company intranet on January 2, 2025.
- Manager briefing sessions held on January 6 and 7 to address questions about unlimited sick days and the intent behind Recharge Days.
- Employee Q&A webinar scheduled for January 15, 2025.

Lisa Chang reported that the HR portal has been updated to reflect the new allowances, and employees can now see their updated PTO balances. She noted that 14 employees had carryover balances exceeding the old 4-day limit, and these have been honored under the transition provisions of the new policy. The new 10-day carryover cap takes effect January 1, 2026.

Aisha Mbeki shared early sentiment data from Slack channels and informal feedback: reception has been overwhelmingly positive, with the Recharge Days and unlimited sick time generating the most enthusiasm. Two concerns were raised by managers about potential misuse of unlimited sick days. Rachel noted that the policy includes a provision for HR review of patterns and that the emphasis should be on trust and responsibility.

Sarah Chen emphasized that the PTO changes are part of the company's broader retention strategy and should be framed as an investment in employee well-being, not a cost.

## 2. Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Metrics

Aisha Mbeki presented the annual DEI metrics for 2024:

- **Gender:** 46% women, 53% men, 1% non-binary (company-wide). At the manager level and above: 41% women, up from 37% in 2023.
- **Ethnicity:** 58% White, 18% Asian, 12% Black, 8% Hispanic/Latino, 4% other/multiracial. Year-over-year change: +2% Hispanic/Latino representation, +1% Black representation.
- **Age:** Median age 34. Distribution is roughly 22% under 30, 51% 30-45, 27% over 45.

Aisha noted that the company's representation numbers are trending in the right direction but remain below the committee's 2025 targets for Hispanic/Latino (target: 10%) and Black (target: 15%) representation. She recommended increasing partnerships with HBCUs and Hispanic-serving institutions for campus recruiting, and expanding the referral bonus to $3,000 for hires that increase underrepresented group representation.

Tom Gallagher agreed and noted that the talent acquisition team is already planning to attend career fairs at 4 new institutions in Q1.

## 3. 2025 Training and Development Budget

Rachel Kim presented the proposed training and development budget for 2025: **$450,000**, a 15% increase over the 2024 budget of $390,000. The breakdown is as follows:

| Category | Allocation |
|----------|-----------|
| Professional certifications and courses | $150,000 |
| Leadership development program | $80,000 |
| Technical skills training (including AI upskilling) | $120,000 |
| Conference attendance | $60,000 |
| Internal programming (Lunch & Learn, mentorship) | $40,000 |

The largest increase is in technical skills training, reflecting the company's AI initiative and the need to upskill existing staff on AI tools and workflows. Rachel noted that Maria Rodriguez's team has identified specific training needs for engineers, and the budget accounts for external courses, platform licenses, and internal workshop facilitation.

Sarah Chen approved the budget and asked Rachel to coordinate with Maria on the AI training timeline to ensure alignment with the AI initiative's 90-day milestone.

## 4. Employee Engagement Survey Results

Aisha Mbeki shared highlights from the annual employee engagement survey, conducted in November 2024 with a 78% response rate:

- Overall engagement score: **4.1 out of 5** (up from 3.9 in 2023).
- Highest-rated areas: team collaboration (4.4), manager effectiveness (4.3), and work-life balance (4.2).
- Lowest-rated areas: career advancement clarity (3.5), cross-department communication (3.6), and compensation competitiveness (3.7).

The committee discussed the career advancement finding and agreed that the leadership development program and clearer promotion criteria (to be published in Q1) should help address this concern. David Park suggested adding a mid-year pulse survey to track progress.

## 5. Open Items

Tom Gallagher raised the question of whether the company should formalize a return-to-office exception process for employees with documented medical or caregiving needs. Rachel agreed to draft a proposal for the next meeting.

Lisa Chang noted that open enrollment for 2025 benefits closes January 31 and reminded the committee to encourage employees to review their options.
